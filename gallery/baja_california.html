  • Valle De los Cirios
  • The cirios, or boojum trees in English, are tall, bendy, often awkwardly leaning plants up to 20m tall. They exist nowhere else on Earth and although they poke like a cactus, they are actually trees because of the woody steam and height. They look like they came right out of a Dr. Seuss book!

  • La Paz Sunset rides.
  • Tuly and her husband, Jesus, regularly host multiple bicycle tourists as some of the most active Warm Showers hosts that we have met and we are so grateful for everything they have done to help us. We spent a few days in La Paz, and then flew to work in Peru (me) and Guatemala (Gordon) with Operation Groundswell for a month. They let us store our bikes for free, Tuly drove us to the airport, and we stayed with them for a week when we returned.
  • We met Marcio and Robert at Tuly's house when we returned from our month worked. We decided to go swiming with sharks before catching the ferry together to Mazatlan
  • I will never forget everything Tuly did for us. She has the opinion that if you have the opportunity to help someone, you should. It's that simple and I will work to pay it forward for the rest of my life.
