  • We arrived in a tiny town one night absolutely starving. Silvia owned a small shop and could notice our hunger. She made us a delicious dinner, insisted we camp on her property, prepared us breakfast and packed us a lunch, and refused to take our money. As a single mother to a sweet, responsible, thoughtful young man, we are so grateful for her motherly care for us. . . Llegando en un pueblito chiquito con mucho hambre, entramos en una tiendita donde la dueña, Silvia, se dio cuenta de nuestro hambre. Nos preparó una cena deliciosa, insisto que campemos en su propiedad y también nos preparo un desayuno y un almuerzo para el camino. Y no acepto ningún pagamento. Como madre soltera a un hijo cariño y responsable, que felices estamos que nos trate con tanto cariño maternal.

  • The world needs more people like Nestor! This cheery Argentinian overtook us as we were struggling against a gale-force headwind. He stopped further up the road and waited for us to offer us a ride to the next town. We enjoyed a great ride with he, his wife and his daughter as we shared some maté and plenty of stories!
  • We randomly met Julio and after hearing about our journey, he asked us where we would be staying that night. We said we didn't know and asked if he had suggestions on places to camp. He did even better and invited us to stay with him and his wife Maby. We ended up staying a few nights and could not be more grateful for the time and company (not to mention, the beds, laundry and shower!) We can't wait to see pictures of the baby!! . . Conocimos a Julio durante un descanso y, cuando escucho de nuestro viaje, nos pregunto dónde nos quedaríamos la noche. Dijimos que no se sabía y al escuchar eso, nos invitó de quedarnos con el y con su esposa, Maby. De hecho, nos quedamos tres noches y los agradecemos mucho por la estancia y su compañía (sin mencionar las camas, la lavandería y las duchas!) No vemos la hora de ver fotos del bebé!
